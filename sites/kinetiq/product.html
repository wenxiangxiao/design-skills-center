01 / CAPTURE PIPELINE

DOM event to replay in 38 ms

A 7 KB SDK batches pointer, scroll and touch events on the client, compresses them into motion frames, and ships them to the nearest of 34 edge points of presence. The stream processor reassembles sessions in order — even across tabs, reconnects and flaky hotel wi-fi.

02 / CURSOR FORENSICS

Every hesitation, on the record

Kinetiq samples pointer position at 120 Hz and classifies the geometry: a tight loop is hesitation, repeated clicks inside 24 px within 900 ms is a rage cluster, a straight sprint to the corner is an exit vector. You don't watch four minutes of video — you jump straight to the seven seconds that matter.

One number your roadmap obeys

It is a weighted blend of hesitation time, rage input, scroll thrash and dead taps — tuned against 11 million labelled sessions. Teams pin it to dashboards and treat a rise like a failing test.
